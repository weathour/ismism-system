## Core thesis

ISMISM does not treat governance, law, bureaucracy, and order as neutral background institutions. This layer shows a chain: rules and legal forms present themselves as objective order; bureaucracy and administration distribute responsibility through procedures; discipline, command, and obedience produce institutional subject-positions; risk/security management justifies control; justice and legitimacy imagination then let rule, procedure, law, or organization be mistaken for practical transformation.

## Diagnostic sequence

1. **Carrier** — law, bureaucracy, institution, administrative apparatus, organization, committee, state machine, public order.
2. **Mechanism** — rule, procedure, command, discipline, governance technology, management, risk/security control, legality reproduction.
3. **Subject-position** — institution-facing petitioner, obedient member, rule-follower, bureaucratic functionary, public-judgment participant, committee/organization cadre.
4. **Fantasy / misrecognition** — rule is mistaken for justice; legality for legitimacy; procedure for truth; security for liberation; organization for practice by itself.
5. **Contradiction** — the same forms that stabilize order also displace responsibility, freeze action, produce Kafka-like judgment, and hide power networks.

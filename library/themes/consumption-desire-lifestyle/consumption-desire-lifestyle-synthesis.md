## Core diagnostic claim

Consumption in this corpus is not merely buying. It is a diagnostic chain:

**consumerism / commodity / lifestyle scene → desire and enjoyment subject → market or symbolic carrier → fantasy of happiness, identity, or value → contradiction of uneven accumulation and constrained enjoyment.**

The anchor row is row 7, where `科学消费主义` names a regime in which happiness and enjoyment become countable and accumulable (`ev:consumption:0007:01`, `ev:consumption:0007:02`, `ev:consumption:0007:03`). Row 6 supplies the neighboring evolutionary/consumerist movement (`ev:consumption:0006:01`, `ev:consumption:0006:02`), and row 337 ties consumption to capital realization (`ev:consumption:0337:01`).

## Diagnostic grammar

1. **Phenomenon** — consumerism appears as `科学消费主义`, market enjoyment, lifestyle display, advertising/marketing engineering, and commodity fetishism.
2. **Subject-position** — the consumer is not sovereign; the subject is placed as a producer of desire and an experiencer of market enjoyment (`term:消费主体:*`, `term:消费者体验:*`).
3. **Mechanism** — science/industry/market/experience are chained together; advertising and platform marketing engineer choices (`ev:consumption:0021:01`–`03`).
4. **Fantasy / misrecognition** — commodity fetishism and happiness accumulation misrecognize social relations as object-value or measurable enjoyment (`ev:consumption:0034:01`, `ev:consumption:0275:01`).
5. **Carrier** — commodity, money, market, price, lifestyle, brand/luxury signs, and emotional-management discourse.
6. **Contradiction** — enjoyment is promised as accumulation but is uneven, constrained, and often guilty or repressed (`ev:consumption:0007:20`–`24`).
7. **Practical opening** — the diagnostic use is to separate personal desire from the market fantasy that makes desire appear as a consumer destiny.

## Cross-theme bridges

- **Capitalism**: capital realization requires consumption and market realization; use rows 337, 338, 339, 248.
- **Psychoanalysis-Subjectivity**: desire/enjoyment is a bridge, but not all desire is consumption; use only evidence-backed bridge rows such as 85, 111, 262, 347.
- **Aesthetics-Media**: advertising, narrative, image, lifestyle, and brand display create consumption scenes; use rows 53, 259, 294.
- **Labor / Family / Education**: consumption can mediate reproduction and status, but G013 does not make claims without row-level evidence.

## Boundaries

- `情绪消费`, `身份消费`, and `自我品牌化` are operational diagnostic markers; the corpus often gives adjacent exact terms (`情绪`, `身份`, `品牌`, `广告`, `营销`, `生活方式`) rather than contemporary labels.
- Do not turn the layer into marketing advice, moral condemnation of consumers, or current-events commentary.

## Core diagnostic

The Family theme treats family and intimacy not as private moral facts, and not as external family-sociology commentary. It makes visible how the corpus links 家庭、婚姻、父母/孩子、爱情、爱欲、父权/夫权/族权、妇女/儿童解放 and 社会再生产 into a social-phenomenon diagnostic grammar:

`family/intimacy phenomenon → parent/child/partner/gendered subject-position → reproduction or kinship mechanism → romance/father/family fantasy → household/marriage/kinship carrier → contradiction → practice opening`.

Evidence anchors include `ev:family:0093:01` for family-internal symbolic reproduction, `ev:family:0055:01` for modern romance ideology, `ev:family:0330:01` for economic-women-children triple liberation, and `ev:family:0363:04` for regeneration and reproduction.

## Diagnostic payoffs

1. **Family as reproduction mechanism**: rows 93, 322, 325, 337, and 363 show family/reproduction as more than biological continuation; it reproduces symbolic systems, life materials, hope, kinship, and social relations.
2. **Romance as ideology**: rows 45, 55, 85, and 184 prevent “爱情” from being treated as a purely private feeling. Love can become religion, fantasy of completion, or a contradiction inside marriage/intimacy.
3. **Gendered domestic order**: rows 61, 90, 257, 327, 329, and 330 bind family diagnosis to feminism/social reproduction without collapsing into a generic gender encyclopedia.
4. **Practice opening**: rows 327 and 330 anchor liberation not in slogan but in concrete breaks with father/husband/clan authority, household dependence, and the family binding of women and children.

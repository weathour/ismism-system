## Core thesis

The theme reads psychological distress as a social-symptom surface. In the corpus, anxiety, pain, despair, nihilism, addictive enjoyment, repression, trauma, and pathological naming are not treated as private mental facts alone. They become diagnostic when a row shows a passage from social contradiction, symbolic demand, desire, institutional sorting, market enjoyment, or blocked practice into a subject's lived suffering.

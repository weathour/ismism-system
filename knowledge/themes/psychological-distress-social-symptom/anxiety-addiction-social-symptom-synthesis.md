# Anxiety / Addiction / Social Symptom Synthesis

- status: focused draft synthesis
- boundary: corpus-grounded social diagnosis only; not clinical or therapeutic advice.

## Pattern

Anxiety names the subject's inability to stabilize a future, an order, or a demand. Addiction names the way relief becomes repetitive capture. Social symptom names the shared structure that lets private pain express a public contradiction.

In this theme, anxiety and addiction should be read together: anxiety destabilizes the subject; enjoyment promises relief; repetition converts relief into a mechanism. The result is not a medical claim about a person, but a matrix reading of how ISMISM lets the private affect become evidence of a social form.

## Evidence path

`anxiety-depression-distress` rows should be checked first for affective pressure; `addiction-enjoyment-compulsion` rows for repetitive enjoyment; `symptom-pathology-medicalization` rows for naming and management. The relation layer then tracks transitions such as anxiety → enjoyment, repression → symptom, symptom → treatment, and social contradiction → private suffering.
